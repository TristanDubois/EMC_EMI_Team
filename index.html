Few words

The EMC/EMI Team belongs to the Reliability Team of the IMS Laboratory.

The EMC/EMI Team performed advanced researches in EMC domaine and more specifically on:

  • the susceptibility issues,
  • the IEMI (Intentional ElectroMagnetic Interference) threat.

Their work focus on simulation and experimental approches in order to understand the disturbance phenomena appearing when electronic circuits are subjected to medium and high amplitude electromagnectic inteferences. Moreover, the EMC team aims to developp new test benches allowing to perform better EMC characterization and extract EMC models.

Experimental Equipments and Software Used

The experimental equipments available at the IMS laboratory, allow to performed both radiated and conducted emissivity and immunity studies at electronic component and board level. For measurement and EMC characterization at equipment level the anechoic chamber located at the ENSEIRB/MATMECA Engineering School can be used. The EMC team of IMS Laboratory have acces to the following test benches and equipments:

  • Direct Power Injection (DPI) test bench (100 kHz - 6 GHz / 25 W)
  • Bulk Current Injection (BCI) test bench (100 kHz - 1 GHz / 15 W)
  • High Voltage Burst test bench (100 ns - 4000 V)
  • TEM/GTEM Cells with electric field probe (100 kHz - 6 GHz / 25 W and 800 MHz - 1000 MHz / 100 W and 1800 MHz - 2000 MHz / 50 W)
  • Near Field Scan test bench (E and H probes)
